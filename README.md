# Technical and Management Ladders

This repository presents an engineering performance management framework as two career tracks:

* [**Technical Track**](Technical.html): individual contributor growth across `P1` through `P7`
* [**Management Track**](Management.html): people-leadership growth across `M1` through `M6`

The Technical track follows a Professional Track model and uses `P` level identifiers. The Management track follows a Management Track model and uses `M` level identifiers.

The framework is meant to support career conversations, performance discussions, and promotion readiness. It should be used as guidance, not as a checklist.

# How To Use This Framework

Use the track pages to anchor conversations around:

* scope of work
* consistency of performance
* evidence of next-level behavior
* likely growth areas for the next review cycle

Promotion readiness should be based on sustained next-level performance, not on a one-time success or a single project. Evidence can come from:

* manager conversations
* peer and cross-functional feedback
* self-assessment
* delivery outcomes and business impact

# Leveling Dimensions

Both tracks use the same five dimensions so that every level can be reviewed in a consistent way:

* **Knowledge & Application**: depth of expertise, business understanding, and ability to apply judgment
* **Leadership & Influence**: ability to guide direction, lead work, and shape decisions through expertise or formal authority
* **Impact & Accountability**: scale of ownership, time horizon, and consequences of the work
* **Problem Solving & Judgement**: complexity of problems, ambiguity of context, and quality of decision-making
* **Collaboration & Interaction**: ability to communicate, persuade, align stakeholders, and build relationships

# Track Summaries

## Technical Track

The Technical track is built for people whose primary impact comes through their own technical work. As levels increase, the center of gravity shifts:

* from learning and execution to deep expertise
* from local delivery to functional and company-wide impact
* from solving known problems to setting technical direction and shaping strategy

Read the full ladder on the [Technical track](Technical.html).

## Management Track

The Management track is built for people whose primary impact comes through others. As levels increase, the center of gravity shifts:

* from team supervision to multi-layer organizational leadership
* from local operations to longer-range functional strategy
* from day-to-day coordination to company-shaping decisions

Read the full ladder on the [Management track](Management.html).

# Progression Notes

Some levels are naturally broader and more selective than others:

* `P3` is the journey-level position in the Technical track
* progression into the highest Technical levels is constrained by business need, individual capability, and formal approval gates
* Management levels expand through larger teams, broader budgets, longer planning horizons, and more strategic accountability

The framework describes the expected shape of a level, but people may still be stronger in some dimensions than in others. That is normal and should inform development plans, not short-circuit thoughtful review.
